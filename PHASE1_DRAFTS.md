### 4. HOW YOU LEAD (Team leadership philosophy)

```
I lead through clarity, not control. My approach:

**Make decisions visible.** Most team friction comes from unclear decision-making 
processes, not disagreement about outcomes. I design governance that surfaces 
"why," not just "what."

**Build for delegation.** The goal is always to give your teams more autonomy, 
not less. That means designing systems they trust and can execute independently.

**Optimize for learning.** Every decision is a chance to learn. I favor 
frameworks that are transparent (easy to audit, improve, evolve) over hidden 
intelligence (smart decisions that no one understands).

**Cross-functional by default.** Product doesn't live in a silo. I work closely 
with design, engineering, data, and commercial teams—and I expect them to be 
full decision partners, not approval gates.
```

---

### 5. WHY THESE FOUR CASES MATTER TOGETHER (The through-line)

```
These cases are connected by one thread: How do product teams make better 
decisions at enterprise scale?

Each case tackles a different bottleneck. PI planning felt chaotic, so I built 
a decision system. Specs were slow, so I built a workflow. Stakeholder alignment 
was messy, so I built a governance model. And when new AI capabilities appeared, 
I applied the same principle: build a system, not heroics.

Together, they show a progression: Decision frameworks → Workflows → Governance 
Models → Risk Management. Each one builds on the last. And each one is grounded 
in the belief that the best product teams aren't the ones with the smartest PM. 
They're the ones with the clearest process.
```

---

### 6. OPERATING PRINCIPLES (Core beliefs in action)

```
**Process beats sophistication.** A simple, consistent decision model will 
outperform a perfect one that's hard to follow. Repeatability wins.

**Governance accelerates, not slows.** When teams trust the process, they 
embrace it. Safety and speed aren't tradeoffs; they're reinforcing.

**Transparency builds alignment.** Most disagreement comes from hidden 
assumptions, not true difference of opinion. Make decision logic visible, 
and alignment follows.

**Constraints are features.** Enterprise environments are constraint-heavy 
(compliance, legacy systems, distributed teams). The best products are built 
*with* constraints, not despite them.

**Scale through systems.** Heroic PMs don't scale. Systems scale. Build the 
operating model, not the individual decisions.
```

## CASE METRICS: FINAL VERSION (Ready to implement)

### Case 1: PM Intelligence Platform
```
**What Changed:**
Prioritization cycles went from open-ended 2-PI planning → scoped 15-day 
decision cycle. Inputs now flow through a standard framework instead of 
scattered Slack discussions.

**Scale:** 
Impacted 12-person product team, enabling 4 parallel workstreams

**Key Outcomes:**
- Planning cycle: From 2+ weeks exploratory → 15-day scoped decision
- Prioritization consistency: Increased from 60% cross-team alignment → 90%+
- Downstream clarity: Engineering estimates improved by ~40%
```

### Case 2: PM Workflow Automation
```
**What Changed:**
Spec writing, epic/feature creation, and done review shifted from async 
friction points → standardized, tooled workflows that reduced review cycles.

**Scale:**

**Key Outcomes:**
- Spec review turnaround: From 4–8 hours per cycle → ~2 hours
- Epic/feature creation: From 3–4 days → 1 day
- Done review consistency: From 50% complete → 90%+ stakeholder alignment
- Team adoption: 95% usage after 6 weeks
```
